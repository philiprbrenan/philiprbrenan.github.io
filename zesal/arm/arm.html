Executive Summary

Hi every-one, my name is Vanina Godoy . I work on product development at Appa Apps where we are designing "Database on a chip": High speed, low power database look-ups to reduce Data Center electricity bills for real money cost savings.

Why do we want to do this?

The world is warming. The Guardian reports that we are likely to pass 1.5 degrees centigrade within the next three years.

The Wall Street Journal reports that Data Centers consume one hundred times more power than that of a typical industrial business.

According to the International Energy Agency: Data Centers, globally, consume as much energy as the entire electricity usage of medium-sized countries like Spain, Mexico, Argentina, or Britain.

David Suzuki tells us that: The most effective way to save energy is to stop wasting it.

Data Centers waste a lot of time and energy doing database look-ups using software running on generic CPUs.

We believe we can significantly reduce the amount of energy used to do database look-ups by replacing the software, currently used to do them, with specialized chips designed to implement the same B-Tree algorithm more efficiently in hardware instead. We are following the approach outlined in Introduction to VLSI by Carver Mead and Lynn Conway, written in 1980 by designing CPU instructions optimized for database look-ups.

Graphics processing units show us the way: they are present in every smartphone today because they burn one thousand times less power than the equivalent software running on a generic CPU while running one thousand times faster. We can do the same with database look-ups.

Burning less power to do each look-up means: using less electricity; which means: lower electricity bills for Data Center operators; and: less carbon dioxide in the atmosphere from burning fossil fuels to generate that electricity; so: we can all breathe free; while Data Center customers waste less time waiting for their look-ups. Everybody wins!

Problem Statement

Data Centers consume 100 times more power than a typical industrial business. Wall Street Journal 2024-03-29 A14

Electricity demand to power Data Centers is projected to increase by 13% to 15% compounded annually through 2030. Yet a shortage of power is already delaying new Data Centers by two to six years, according to commercial- real estate firm CBRE Group. It is also driving Big Tech companies into the energy business. Amazon this month struck a $650 million deal to buy a Data Center in Pennsylvania powered by an on-site 2.5 gigawatt nuclear plant. Wall Street Journal 2024-03-29 A14

Service and Business Model

High speed, low power database system on a chip reduces power consumption for Data Centers leading to reduced electricity bills which saves real money making for an easy sale of the chips that make these saving possible.

A GPU saves power by performing graphics calculations in hardware rather than in software. Likewise Bitcoin saves power by performing hash calculations in hardware rather then software.

Nvidia has become a trillion dollar company by providing the masks for GPU chips that perform graphics functions via custom Silicon. This process of customizing chips to specific tasks was predicted in Introduction to VLSI by Carver Mead and Lynn Conway, written in 1980 .

Eventually this process will be extended to database look ups as these too consume significant amounts of time and power in Data Center. Not only does the lookup burn energy on the database server, but also on the computers waiting for responses from the database.

All the major database systems are open source. By making sample chips available with these capabilities to open source developers we can reasonable expect them to take advantage of these capabilities just as, for example, gcc takes advantage of vector instructions to implement the strlen function whenever possible.

The availability of databases capable of exploiting such capabilities will lead to more demand for more access to such chips which Data Center operators will be keen to provide as they will be able to save money on their electricity bills while making their offering more compelling than those who fail to offer such a service. An analogous process has already led to the replacement of mechanical disk drives with Solid State Drive in Data Centers for similar reasons.

Competitive Landscape

Field Programmable Gate Arrays

An fpga could also be programmed to do fast database look ups using the B-Tree algorithm. However, fpga devices use 10-100 times as much power as a custom chip and are perhaps 10-100 times lower. For database look ups, an fpga does not save enough electricity or time to make it a viable solution.

Single Instruction, Multiple Data

SIMD vector instruction sets are common these days. We emulated the X86-64 instruction set using NasmX86 to see if SIMD instructions produced any useful gain in the implementation of the B-Tree algorithm. On the contrary, we found that using SIMD instruction produced a slower implementation because more time was spent assembling the data in the formats required by these instructions than was gained by using them.

Custom Computers

Both Oracle and IBM have custom computer products that indicate that they have given thought to this problem.

DB2 with BLU Acceleration runs on existing IBM infrastructure. It is a software solution not a chip level hardware solution.

Oracle Exadata is a conventional computer optimized with appropriate memory, disk drives and network options to provide an optimal conventional computing environment in which to run an Oracle database. But there is no evidence of any new chip design being used to power this solution.

Both of these important database vendors are aware that acceleration is beneficial to database processing but they have not moved beyond the constraints of conventional solutions. Given that both companies operate in mature markets, a break through on this area would surely have to be of great interest to them.

Solution

Solution Outline

One of the more common actions performed by computers in Data Centers is running software to look up data in a database or in memory data structures.

Convention CPUs have vector instruction sets specialized for doing arithmetic but do not have instructions specialized for traversing database structures held in memory. Consequently such looks ups consume much more time and electricity than is strictly necessary.

Converting software to hardware can speed up processing by a factor of one thousand while similarly reducing power requirements. This has already been demonstrated by the rapid adoption of GPUs for smart phone graphics processing and the application specific integrated circuits used for crypto currency mining.

Following the approach outlined in Introduction to VLSI by Carver Mead and Lynn Conway, written in 1980 we are designing instructions optimized for database look ups. These instructions will then be executed by a small CPU against a database held in Solid State Drive memory to obtain nano second look ups while using less power than conventional CPUs. Much less time times much less power is much less energy.

Communication with the outside world will be via on chip, high speed Ethernet controllers.

End application

All database systems in Data Centers are addressed via IP address addresses. It will be easy to plug the chip into an Ethernet controller to act as a front end cache for such systems to perform high speed, low power look ups.

Technology

The well known process of converting software to hardware as described in Introduction to VLSI by Carver Mead and Lynn Conway, written in 1980 .

High-level solution architecture

All digital computing can be reduced to a sequence of operations implemented by Nand digital logic gates. A conventional, generic CPU most manufacture this sequence on the fly as needed as one sequence among many. Database on a Chip would avoid all this overhead by hard coding the well known B-Tree algorithm used in most database look ups as a sequence of digital logic gates on a Silicon chip implementing the most intensive part of the B-Tree look up algorithm directly in the fabric of the chip in the most efficient manner possible.

These instruction sequences would be placed either on their own chip and connected via Ethernet or included as part of existing system on a chip such as Qualcomm Snapdragon X Elite.

Solution Unique Selling Point

Plug the chip into an Ethernet router and watch it do database look ups faster while using less electricity than your current equipment can. Less power consumption means more profits.

Solution development

Roadmap

Currently we have succeeded in representing the B-Tree look up algorithm as an instruction implemented in Nand digital logic gates.

We used a purpose built Electronic Design Automation tool: Silicon Chip to design, simulate, and layout the required sequences of digital logic gates. This solution needs to be cross checked with industry standard tools to confirm expected operation.

Then we need to place this sequence on a real chip alongside a generic CPU and Ethernet controller so that we can drive these specialized instructions to see if they lead to the expected improvements.

Key challenge

Getting our proposed wiring diagram implemented as a functioning chip.

Arm IP

The additional instructions implemented by database on a chip could usefully be driven by a conventional low power generic CPU either directly on the same chip or connected to such CPUs on other chips via an Ethernet controller.
