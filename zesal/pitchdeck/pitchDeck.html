Problem or Market Opportunity

High speed, low power database system on a chip. The need for faster graphics produced the gpu industry. The need to secure crypto currencies produced the bitcoin miner industry. Most commercial data processing is about looking stuff up. There is an opportunity to build application specific integrated circuit chips that do database look ups and sell them to Cloud Computing companies on the basis that they will lower their electricity bills.

Solution/Product to be Developed

An application specific integrated circuit mask for a chip dedicated to high speed data look up and a working chip produced from that mask. Obviously a working Silicon sample whose performance could be measured would be a crucial proof that Database on a Chip has value.

Use of Semiconductors in Solution

Layers of flash memory and Static Random Access Memory integrated in a vertical stack with close to memory processing using conventional logic digital logic gates to perform high speed database look ups on the stored data. Chips connected to each other via optical Ethernet in parallel to provide scale and at Tera Herz transmission rates with the outside world.

Current or Expected Customers

Cloud Computing data centers with large electricity bills.

Potential Market Size

Perhaps savings on the order of $100 million per year.

According to the IEA world production of electricity is: 1e13 KWh per year. Data centers use about 1% of that electricity. Say we could reduce data center electricity usage by 1% that would save 10e9 KWh annually. A KWh costs approximately US$1e-1. This would yield a gross savings of US$1e8 per year.

Assuming that the price of operating a chip is $10 per chip per year and that we need 1e6 chips to support this reduction is electricity usage, then there are potential savings of US$90 million per year. Splitting the savings with the Cloud Computing data center industry ensures that they make savings of US$45 million per year which might be enough to incentivise them to use this technology while leaving room for US$45 million in profits annually for the mask rights holder.

The preceding deals only with power savings. Being able to look things up faster than a competitor might well bestow unexpected benefits which might make such chips valuable in as yet unknown applications, perhaps, for example: analyzing the movements of the stock market. If such unexpected benefits did materialize then the capability to produce such chips at scale would be that much more the profitable.

Describe Your Business Model

Payments for the right to use the mask describing the application specific integrated circuit to produce chips for sales to Cloud Computing data centers.

Competitors & Alternatives

The alternative is to continue to use generic CPUs to look up data in conventional memory. While Moore's Law was still in effect it was too expensive to develop specialist techniques for data look up because the generic look up techniques provided by conventional CPUs were improving so rapidly. Now that Moore's Law has run its course there is opening for such specialist solutions.

Competitive Advantage

The very real possibility of saving real money in real Cloud Computing centers by reducing the electricity needed to perform database look ups.

Intellectual Property

Mask for application specific integrated circuit implementing Database on a Chip.

Go to Market (Marketing & Sales) Strategy

Send a fax to each of the major Cloud Computing data center operators - Amazon Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud Platform etc. explaining the benefits with a sample chip mounted on a printed circuit board for them to test by plugging it into an Ethernet router.

Copies to Oracle to keep them appraised of the situation.

Feasibility Demonstration

Plug a circuit board with such a chip mounted on it into an Ethernet router and show that it quickly starts to answer queries quickly.
